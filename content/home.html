About

I am a PhD student in computer science at Michigan State University working under the supervision of Dr. Laura Dillon. I am researching test data generation from conceptual data models expressed in the Object Role Modeling language. More broadly, my research interests include software engineering, database theory, and model-based development.

Prior to graduate school, I worked for 10 years in industry as a software engineer primarily focused on SAS development. Most recently, I was a Senior Clinical Programmer at United BioSource, developing SAS programs to analyze clinical trial data and produce reports for submission to the FDA. Prior to that, I developed SAS software for EDS/Hewlett-Packard to analyze automotive warranty data.

For more detail on my professional experience, please see my resume, CV, or LinkedIn profile. I also have a Github account and occasionally participate on Stack Overflow.

In my spare time I enjoy hiking, reading, playing Go, listening to music, and spending time with my wife and children, with whom I live in southeast Michigan.
