About

Fascination with the magic of video and computer games as a child sparked my interest in programming: I wanted to learn how the games worked, how to create worlds of my own. By middle school I started teaching myself to program, and I soon discovered the joy of making things using nothing but logic as the material. As Brooks wrote:

The programmer, like the poet, works only slightly removed from pure thought-stuff. He builds his castles in the air, from air, creating by exertion of the imagination...Yet the program construct, unlike the poet's words, is real in the sense that it moves and works, producing visible outputs separately from the construct itself.

The sense of deep satisfaction that comes from making things is shared by many professions, from carpenters and artists to engineers and novelists. Programming can be incredibly rewarding, and if you do not already know how to program, I'd invite you to learn.

As a programmer, I am passionate about crafting elegant code: code that correctly solves complex problems simply, cleanly, and clearly. Principles such as DRY and YAGNI guide my work. I am idealistic, but I am not a purist: ultimately, code is a means to an end, and it is the finished product which truly matters.

I currently work as a clinical programmer / analyst, writing SAS programs to analyze and report clinical trial data in the oncology and cardiovascular therapeutic areas. But, I also have experience with languages such as VB.NET, SQL, and C; web technologies such as HTML, CSS, JSP, and JavaScript; and scripting languages such as Korn shell script.

In my spare time I enjoy hiking, reading, playing Go, listening to music, and spending time with my wife and children, with whom I live in southeast Michigan.
