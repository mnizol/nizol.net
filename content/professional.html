Recommended Reading

Technical Books

  • Code Complete, 2nd Edition by Steve McConnell. Contains advice that every working programmer should know. All graduating seniors from a computer science program should be required to read this book prior to starting their first job.
  • The C Programming Language by Brian Kernighan and Dennis Ritchie. This book is a model of what a programming language book should be. It is concise (about 250 pages including a formal grammar of the C language), clear and, frankly, fun to read.
  • Algorithmics by David Harel. Algorithms are the heart of programming. This book explores topics such as data structures, fundamental algorithms, complexity, and Turing machines in an accessible and enjoyable manner.

Non-Technical Books

  • Getting Things Done by David Allen. Very popular as a productivity methodology among programmers; following only a few key principles of the book can be sufficient to get (and keep) yourself organized.
  • The Emperor's New Mind by Roger Penrose. Penrose is a contemporary of Hawking, and writes an incredible book that traces the underpinnings of computers and the human mind--from Calculus and Quantum Physics to Neurobiology--in an attempt to answer the question of whether artificial intelligence in the strong sense is possible (spoiler: Penrose doesn't think so). It's a fascinating read written at a non-technical level.

On the Web

  • Paul Graham: Essays: by the creator of the start-up incubator, Y-Combinator.
  • Knowing and Doing: Thought-provoking blog posts by Eugene Wallingford, head of the Department of Computer Science at the University of Northern Iowa.
  • Send More Paramedics: blog of Michael Fogus, co-author of The Joy of Clojure; Clojure is a dialect of Lisp.
  • Stack Overflow: Addictive question and answer website for programmers. Listserv for the 21st century.
  • SAS-L: Despite sites like Stack Overflow, listservs still thrive in some communities; all of the best SAS programmers--Whitlock, Dorfman, Fehd, Carpenter--are to be found posting on SAS-L.
